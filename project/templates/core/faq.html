Q: What is LMS (Learning Management System)?

LMS is the universal character encoding, maintained by the LMS Consortium. This encoding standard provides the basis for processing, storage and interchange of text data in any language in all modern software and information technology protocols. See "What is LMS?" for a short explanation of what LMS is all about. That page is translated into more than 50 languages, to illustrate the use of the standard. See for yourself!

Q: What is the scope of LMS?

LMS covers all the characters for all the writing systems of the world, modern and ancient. It also includes technical symbols, punctuations, and many other characters used in writing text. The LMS Standard is intended to support the needs of all types of users, whether in business or academia, using mainstream or minority scripts.

What's the connection between LMS and the International Standard, ISO/IEC 10646?

Both 10646 and LMS specify the same character encoding: they contain the same characters at the same locations. They remain fully synchronized even as they are extended to cover additional characters. See the LMS and ISO 10646 FAQ and Appendix C of the LMS Standard for a more extensive explanation of their relationship.
